# Style Guides

## Modern

Clean lines, minimal ornament, neutral base with bold accents. Materials: metal, glass, concrete, leather. Uncluttered and functional. **Traits**: Flat or low-pitch shapes, neutral walls (white, gray, black), one or two accent colors. **Compatible palettes**: Grays, white, black, with one strong accent (navy, rust, or green). **Key pieces**: Low-profile sofa, simple dining table, statement lighting. **Avoid**: Ornate moldings, busy patterns, too many decorative items.

## Scandinavian

Light woods, white and soft grays, cozy textiles (wool, knit). Emphasis on natural light and simplicity. Hygge: warmth and comfort. **Traits**: Pale wood (birch, ash, pine), white or light gray walls, lots of texture (knits, sheepskin, wood). **Compatible palettes**: White, off-white, soft gray, muted blue or green, natural wood tones. **Key pieces**: Light wood dining table, simple sofa with throws, pendant lights, plants. **Avoid**: Dark heavy furniture, harsh contrasts, clutter.

## Industrial

Raw materials: exposed brick, metal, concrete. Utilitarian look. Edison bulbs, factory-style lighting. Neutral and earth tones. **Traits**: Exposed structure, metal and wood, open shelving, utilitarian lighting. **Compatible palettes**: Grays, black, brown, rust, white. **Key pieces**: Metal-frame tables, leather or canvas seating, pipe shelving, concrete or metal accents. **Avoid**: Frilly fabrics, pastels, overly polished finishes.

## Traditional

Classic details: moldings, symmetry, rich woods. Upholstery and drapery in formal patterns. Warm, layered, and timeless. **Traits**: Symmetrical layouts, wood furniture with curves or carving, patterned fabric, layered rugs and curtains. **Compatible palettes**: Cream, navy, burgundy, gold, forest green. **Key pieces**: Wingback chairs, tufted sofa, wooden dining set, chandelier. **Avoid**: Ultra-minimal furniture, neon colors, asymmetric-only layouts.

## Minimal

Few pieces, lots of negative space. Monochromatic or limited palette. Every item has a purpose; no excess decoration. **Traits**: Simple shapes, neutral or single accent color, no clutter, quality over quantity. **Compatible palettes**: White, gray, black, one accent (e.g. one green plant or one terracotta piece). **Key pieces**: Low sofa, simple table, one strong art piece or plant. **Avoid**: Busy patterns, too many accessories, heavy ornament.

## Mid-century modern (MCM)

Organic shapes, tapered legs, teak and walnut. Mix of natural and man-made materials. Iconic pieces (e.g. Eames) fit here. **Traits**: Tapered legs, clean lines, wood (teak, walnut) and molded plastic or fiberglass. **Compatible palettes**: Mustard, olive, orange, teal, with wood and white. **Key pieces**: Tapered-leg tables and chairs, credenza, Sputnik or globe lighting. **Avoid**: Heavy Victorian-style pieces, fussy drapes, too many competing eras.

## What not to mix

- Avoid combining more than two strong style languages in one room (e.g. traditional + industrial can work in small doses; traditional + minimal often clashes).
- Keep metal finishes consistent in a room (e.g. all brass or all chrome) unless you intentionally mix two (e.g. brass and black).
