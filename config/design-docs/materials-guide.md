# Materials Guide

## Fabric durability

- **Cotton**: Soft, breathable; good for low-traffic upholstery. Can fade in sun.
- **Linen**: Cool, textured; wrinkles. Best for casual or layered looks.
- **Polyester blends**: Durable, stain-resistant; good for families and high use.
- **Velvet**: Rich look; holds color well. Use in moderate-traffic areas.
- **Leather**: Durable, ages well. Full-grain is highest quality; bonded is budget-friendly.

**Performance (Martindale rub count)**: For upholstery, 15,000+ rubs is suitable for heavy use (e.g. family sofa); 10,000–15,000 for moderate use; below 10,000 for light or decorative use. Check fabric specs when choosing for high-traffic seating.

## Room suitability

- **High-traffic (living room, entry)**: Choose durable fabrics (polyester blends, performance velvet, leather), hard-wearing rugs, and scratch-resistant wood or laminate.
- **Low-traffic (bedroom, formal dining)**: Silk, linen, and delicate weaves are acceptable.
- **Kids and pets**: Avoid silk and light-colored delicate fabrics; prefer stain-resistant and washable covers, leather, or performance fabrics.
- **Bathrooms and kitchens**: Use moisture-resistant materials; avoid untreated wood and absorbent fabrics.

## Wood types

- **Oak**: Hard, versatile; light to medium tone. Traditional and modern.
- **Walnut**: Dark, rich; mid-century and formal.
- **Pine**: Soft, casual; can dent. Good for country or cottage.
- **Maple**: Hard, light; clean look for modern and Scandinavian.

## Countertop materials

- **Quartz**: Non-porous, low maintenance; wide color range. Good for kitchens and baths.
- **Granite**: Natural variation; needs sealing. Durable and classic.
- **Laminate**: Budget option; many patterns. Less heat- and scratch-resistant.
- **Solid surface**: Seamless, repairable; mid-range cost.

## Price tiers (broad guidance)

- **Budget**: Laminate, polyester-blend upholstery, pine or veneer furniture, vinyl or laminate flooring.
- **Mid-range**: Quartz or solid surface, cotton or performance velvet, oak or maple, engineered wood or mid-tier carpet.
- **Investment**: Full-grain leather, solid hardwood (walnut, cherry), natural stone, wool or high-rub-count upholstery.
