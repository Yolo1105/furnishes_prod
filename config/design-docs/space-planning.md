# Space Planning

## Traffic flow

- Main paths need 36 inches (typical) to 42 inches (major circulation).
- Don't block paths with furniture; keep walkways clear between doors and key areas.
- Allow 30 inches between furniture pieces (e.g. sofa to coffee table) for movement.

**By room type**: In living rooms, the main path is often from entry to another room or to the sofa; keep it at least 36 inches. In bedrooms, preserve a path from the door to the bed and to the closet. In dining rooms, allow 36 inches behind chairs when pulled out. In kitchens, 42 inches between counter and island for a main work aisle.

## Focal points

- Every room benefits from a focal point: fireplace, window, or main wall.
- Arrange seating to face or frame the focal point.
- Anchor the room with the largest piece (sofa, bed) first, then add secondary pieces.

Place the sofa or bed so it addresses the focal point (e.g. facing the fireplace or the best window). Secondary seating can flank or angle toward the same focus so the room feels intentional.

## Furniture groupings

- Group seating for conversation: 8–10 feet across is comfortable for talking.
- Leave 14–18 inches between coffee table and sofa for legroom.
- In bedrooms, allow 24 inches on each side of the bed and 36 inches at the foot.

**Scale**: Match furniture scale to room size. In a small living room, a compact sofa and one or two chairs keep the space from feeling cramped; in a large room, a sectional or multiple seating groups can fill the space. Avoid lining all furniture against the walls; pull pieces into the room to create conversation zones.

## Clearances (quick reference)

- Typical walkway: 36 inches. Major path: 42 inches. Between furniture: 30 inches.
- Bed: 24 inches each side, 36 inches at foot.
- Desk: allow 24–30 inches depth for seating and legroom.
