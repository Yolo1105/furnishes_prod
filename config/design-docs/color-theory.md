# Color Theory for Interior Design

## Color wheel relationships

- **Complementary**: Opposite on the wheel (e.g. blue and orange) — high contrast, use in small doses.
- **Analogous**: Adjacent colors (e.g. blue, blue-green, green) — harmonious, easy to live with.
- **Triadic**: Three colors evenly spaced — vibrant; balance one dominant, two accent.

## Warm vs cool palettes

- **Warm**: Reds, oranges, yellows — cozy, energizing; good for living rooms and dining.
- **Cool**: Blues, greens, purples — calm, spacious; good for bedrooms and bathrooms.

## The 60-30-10 rule

Use 60% dominant color (walls, large furniture), 30% secondary (upholstery, curtains), 10% accent (pillows, art, accessories). Keeps the room balanced and intentional. Apply by room: in a living room, walls and sofa might be the 60% (e.g. warm gray), curtains and a second chair the 30% (navy or camel), and throw pillows and art the 10% (mustard or brass). In a bedroom, walls and bedding dominate; headboard and rug form the 30%; lamps and art the 10%.

## Named palettes (actionable examples)

- **Warm neutral**: Sherwin-Williams Accessible Beige (walls), Benjamin Moore White Dove (trim), accent navy in pillows and one chair. Works in living rooms and bedrooms.
- **Cool modern**: Sherwin-Williams Alabaster (walls), charcoal gray on one accent wall or in furniture, brass or gold in lighting and hardware.
- **Calm retreat**: Soft sage or dusty blue on walls, cream upholstery, white trim, natural wood and greenery for the 10%.

## Colors that change perceived size

- **Make a room feel larger**: Light, cool colors (soft grays, pale blue, white). Mirrors and consistent trim color help.
- **Make a room feel smaller or cozier**: Dark or warm walls (navy, deep green, warm gray). Use in large rooms or accent walls.
- **Ceilings**: White or one shade lighter than walls raises the ceiling; dark ceilings lower it (use in large or high spaces for intimacy).

## Where to use bold color

- Accent walls (one wall in a strong hue), artwork, pillows, and small accessories are low-commitment. Upholstery and rugs are medium; wall color and large furniture are high-commitment. Start bold in the 10% and expand if you love it.
